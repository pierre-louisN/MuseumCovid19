Welcome to the Covid Museum

For the first time it is possible to follow, understand, compare within each country the evolution of an emerging disease, the societal reactions it causes whether they are individual, political, medical, etc. through the digital material produced.

You will participate in taking up this challenge through an experiment which consists in automatically detecting the objects present in a digitized drawing then in classifying them. The design you entrust to us is sent to an automatic shape detection service. There is no human intervention to spot the shapes. The technique used is that of artificial neural networks. It is a mechanism capable of learning from experience how to classify data, even when there are errors. The more we ask to recognize objects, the more efficient the system becomes.

We will produce three classifications of the drawings. One before confinement, the other during confinement and finally a final one after confinement. Behavioral scientists will then be able to compare these classifications and consider that the symbolism of the drawings explains a posteriori the feelings of people during confinement.
